Results and Analysis
No benchmark tasks were given as part of the evaluation plan, though usability goals 1 and 5 were generally achieved, though exceptions such as User 3 exist.. Usability goals 3, 4, and 7 could not be evaluated as they related to the functionality of the system. Users struggled with the remaining usability goals, 2 and 6, due to lack of clarity of what the buttons were supposed to do as well as confusion with the wording of the tasks. Task 3 failed most often due to the wording issue, as users did not understand that they were already on the selection page, while tasks 4 and 5 were sometimes executed with difficulty and required prompting. These will be explained in further detail in the next section.

Test Plan Critique
Expanding on some test procedure wording issues mentioned in the previous section, one of the major issues with the test plan was with the task list users were expected to complete. Users frequently found it difficult to understand what the task was asking of them, especially when combined with some broken prototype components. There were two tasks that seemed to cause particular difficulty. The first is “confirm your choice”, which appears in the wording for tasks 2 and 4. There was no “confirm button”, and simply selecting the song seemed insufficient, so many pressed the “start singing” button and moved onto the next page. The second is “go back to the selection screen”. Users did not seem to understand that they were already on the selection screen, and a few tried navigating to other pages to find it. This task could likely be removed entirely as it has little to do with the interface and to avoid causing confusion.

We also had difficulty administering the post-test questionnaire. This is mainly because many of the questions were focused on the intended functionality of the system, which is not present in the prototype. Users did not know how to answer questions 1, 2, and 6, as they assumed that the user was actually able to use the prototype for its singing functionality. These questions do relate to usability goals 3, 4, and 7, but our group has difficulty understanding the relevance of these goals considering how they don’t address the usability of the interface and that they are used to evaluate a simple prototype.

Continuing with the post-test questionnaire, users were also unclear by what was meant by “parts of the prototype”, unsure if they were supposed to be commenting on its supposed functionality or interface components, which we had to explain. We would recommend adding more questions that focused on the user’s experience navigating and using the prototype, giving it a numerical grade, and seeing if they would recommend it to others.

In the test script, we would recommend making it very clear to the user that they are not being evaluated on their performance. User 1 became visibly stressed over her confusion and started randomly clicking buttons, and had to be assuaged by the evaluator. We would also make it more obvious that this is a prototype, not a fully functional system, as some users became confused over buttons not working as expected. This required for us to modify the test script for the following users, reiterating upon these points.

Overall, while the test procedure and materials related well to the stated usability goals, our group is doubtful of their applicability for this particular deliverable. Many tasks and user materials could also be reworded, to make questions and the purpose of the tasks clearer.
Design Critique
To improve the usability of the interface, we can start by addressing the usability issues identified in the Results and Analysis section. Note that these are just suggestions and may be followed or adjusted as seen fit.

Usability Issue

Solution Suggestions

Rationale

“Start Singing” button is unclear.

Replace “Start Singing” with “Listen to Song”.

This makes it clear that the next page is to listen to the song and see it on the pitch screen, not for the user to start singing.

“Done” button is unclear.

Replace “Done” button on the listening page with “Practice Singing”.


Replace “Done” button on the practice page with “Singing Report”.

This makes it clear that the page after the listening page is the practice page.


This makes it clear that the page after the practice page is the report page.

Forwards arrows conflict with the “Done” button.

Remove forward arrow.

This removes ambiguity as to how to move forward to the next page. The user must press the “Done” button, or whatever it may be replaced by, in order to proceed.

Song choice confirmation.

Include a pop-up modal or confirmation tab on the screen that indicates the selected song.

This improves feedback to the user and helps them feel more certain of their song selection and allows them to change more immediately if they selected incorrectly.

Pitch feedback is unclear

Add a brief walkthrough tutorial/tour option.

Giving users the option to see a walkthrough/tutorial lets users that might be unfamiliar with the interface and feedback format become more comfortable with the system.


Lastly, there are some cosmetic issues brought up by individual users that can potentially be addressed, though they were not widespread enough for us to consider as usability issues. User 2 found the lines indicating pitch “ugly and squiggly”, though this sentiment was not expressed by other users. User 2 also suggested making the blue line green in the reporting screen to make it clearer that this was the correct pitch, and that it was hard to tell whether to follow the blocked-pitch approximation or the line.
